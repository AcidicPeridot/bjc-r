In this activity, you will learn how to make things happen when the user clicks certain parts of the stage background.

There are many reasons to use clicks on the background to run code. Clicking on part of a virtual poster might show additional information. In a game, clicking part of a map could move you there.

Locations on the Snap! stage are described using x and y coordinates. The x coordinate tells you the horizontal position. The y coordinate tells you the vertical position.

You can track the location of your mouse by clicking the checkbox next to the x position and y position reporters in the "Sensing" palette. Then values of those reporters will appear on the stage.

Revisiting Your Plan and Choosing Your Click-Areas

  1. Review your project plan. Where on the background would it make sense for the user to click? What will happen when they click there?
  2. Click the checkboxes next to the x position and y position reporters the in the "Sensing" palette. Watch the values of the x position and y position of the mouse as you move it around the stage.
  3. For each place on the stage background that you want the user to be able to click, write down the maximum and minimum x and y values where the user will be able to click. This rectangle will be your "hot spot" (or click-area).
