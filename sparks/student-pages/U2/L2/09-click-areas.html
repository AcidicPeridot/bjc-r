In this activity, you will decide which parts of the stage background will respond to user clicks.

There are many reasons to use clicks on the stage background to run code. Clicking on part of a virtual poster might show additional information. In a game, clicking part of a map could move you there.

Locations on the Snap! stage are described using x and y coordinates. The x coordinate tells you the horizontal position. The y coordinate tells you the vertical position.

You can track the location of your mouse by clicking the checkbox next to the x position and y position reporters in the "Sensing" palette. Then values of those reporters will appear on the stage.

The picture of the coordinate axes shown on the right is a Snap! stage background. You can add it to your project by clicking the "Stage" icon in the lower right corner of the Snap! window, selecting "Backgrounds..." from the Snap! File menu (), and selecting and importing the "XY Grid."
